<h2 id="about">About Me</h2>

<p>I'm Aditi Pattabhiraman, a Master's student at Stanford University in Aeronautics and Astronautics, specializing in Robotics and AI. I build systems at the intersection of machine learning, control theory, and autonomous flight. My research and coursework focus on machine learning, control systems, and robotics, with emphasis on their applications in autonomous systems.</p>
<p>I graduated from The Cooper Union with a Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering, achieving Summa Cum Laude honors. During my undergraduate years I served as President of the Tau Beta Pi/ASME chapter and pursued a Computer Science minor, which strengthened my technical expertise in software development and data-driven problem solving.</p>
<p>Outside the lab, I have explored the commercial side of autonomy through the Stanford Ignite Program, where I worked on a drone delivery start-up. I am also active in the Stanford community as WIAA Graduate Outreach Chair and a member of the Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Belonging Committee.</p>

<p class="honors-line"><strong>Honors &amp; Recognition:</strong> Tau Beta Pi Graduate Fellowship · WIAA Graduate Outreach Chair · DEIB Committee Member · Summa Cum Laude, The Cooper Union</p>

<h2 id="projects">Projects</h2>

<div class="card">
<div class="card-meta">Current · Stanford Multi-Robot Systems Lab</div>
<div class="card-title">Drone Magnetic Field Survey of Crater</div>

- Conducting drone and magnetometer tests using ROS2 VRPN and MOCAP systems in the Stanford Flight Room
- Analyzing pose and magnetic field data to characterize the drone's magnetic field for an upcoming planetary crater survey
- Collaborating with Dr. Mac Schwager and Dr. Anton Ermakov

</div>

<div class="card">
<div class="card-meta">Multi-Robot Systems Lab (MSL)</div>
<div class="card-title">Quadcopter Controller Training</div>

- Developed an imitation learning pipeline to train a quadcopter to fly trajectories using transformers with CUDA
- Designed neural network architectures including MLPs based on low-level control data
- Evaluated generalization performance across varied flight trajectory datasets

</div>

<div class="card">
<div class="card-meta">Stanford AA222 · Optimization</div>
<div class="card-title">Genetic Algorithm Optimization for Virtual Flight</div>

- Optimized virtual flying creatures using genetic algorithms with tournament selection, crossover, and mutation
- Enhanced flight stability and distance in an 18-dimensional design space using aerodynamic modeling

</div>

<div class="card">
<div class="card-meta">Stanford AA228 · Decision Making Under Uncertainty</div>
<div class="card-title">Can We Win Blackjack?</div>

- Implemented Q-learning and SARSA agents for playing Blackjack with policy visualizations
- Developed optimal policies through reinforcement learning techniques and hyperparameter tuning

</div>

<div class="card">
<div class="card-meta">Advanced Controls · Cooper Union</div>
<div class="card-title">Turtlebot Navigation</div>

- Designed Pure Pursuit and Model Predictive Controllers using Python in a ROS2 environment for Turtlebot4
- Conducted comparative analysis between controllers based on distance error and time efficiency

</div>
